The updatable list of books that worth acquaintance in my opinion. If you read some of this – cool, if not – it doesn't matter, I'm not gonna act like they are essential scrolls of ultimate truth or something.

Elliot Aronson — "The Social Animal" (1972)

A perfect eye-opening book for dedicated STEM-zealots who believe human mind and behavior can not be studied in a "right" way. Compilation of correctly set and totally reproducible experiments actually helpful to understand how individual interacts with society, and to make refutable predictions on how people will behave in the future. Fewer vain assumptions, more actual numbers and references to the sources after each described case.

Simson Garfinkel, Daniel Weise — "The UNIX-Haters Handbook" (1994)

Probably the funniest way to learn more about UNIX(-like) operating system(s). "It may sound like a joke, but it isn't: The Book" – me.
